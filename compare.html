Introduction to irrigation energy

There may be two sources of water:

  1. surface

  2. ground

Ground water will have a 'head' associated with the depth of the well. Both will have 'head' associated with the pump pressure.  

The main components defining the irrigation energy use are:

  1. The pump pressure and, if ground water, the well depth

  2. The energy source for the pumping/moving of water (diesel, electricity, solar, etc.)

  3. The amount of irrigation water applied

FTM equations

The total head, \(H\) is expressed as the sum of the head from the pump pressure and the pumping depth. To calculate the head we therefore just need to do some unit conversions.

First, convert the pump pressure to meters:

# 2.31 ft head / 1 psi
# 0.305 m head / 1 ft head
# pump is 25 psi
press_head <- 25  * 2.31 * 0.305
press_head
[1] 17.61375

Next, convert the pump depth to meters:

# depth  is 200 ft
depth_head <- 200 * 0.305
depth_head
[1] 61

Sum them together :

head <- press_head + depth_head
round(head, 2)
[1] 78.61

This is then used in the pumping energy equation:

$ pump energy = \[energy = \frac{H*C_{pump}*(acre-inches_{applied})*25.4 \frac{mm}{in} * (number-of-acres) * (0.404 \frac{ha}{acre})}{E_{pump}*E_{irrigation}*E_{gearhead}*E_{power-unit}}\]

Where:

\[H = head\]

\[\begin{flushleft} H = head\\ C_{pump} = 0.0979\\ E_{pump} = 0.75\\ E_{irrigation} = 1\\ E_{gearhead} = 0.95\\ E_{power-unit} = 1\end{flushleft}\]
